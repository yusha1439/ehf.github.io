Our Foundation

Decades of Dawah & Digital Outreach

In 1991, Yusuf Estes accepted Islam and immediately wanted to share it with the world. Long before Google and YouTube, he recognized the internet's power to spread the message of Islam — and began building websites from scratch.

In 2008, while on a charitable project in the UK, Sheikh Yusuf discovered 14 Muslim-owned TV stations. When asked how many existed in the USA, the answer was zero. By 2011, the Estes Heritage Foundation launched Guide US TV — America's first Islamic satellite television channel — opening with a 48-hour live broadcast.

Today the foundation runs one of the largest English-language Islamic digital media networks in the world: broadcast television, streaming apps, and over 116 active websites reaching millions every month.

The Man Behind the Mission

Why Would a Preacher
Enter Islam?

Sheikh Yusuf Estes

Founder · National Muslim Chaplain · Former US Bureau of Prisons Chaplain

Born as "Skip" Estes in the American Midwest · Music minister & Christian preacher · Piano & organ store owner · Texas, Oklahoma & Florida · Accepted Islam 1991 · Delegate to UN Peace Conference for Religious Leaders

Born into Christianity

My name is Yusuf Estes now — but friends once called me Skip. I was born into a strong Christian family in the Midwest, baptized at 12 in Pasadena, Texas, and spent decades as a music minister and preacher of the Gospel. My father and I built music stores, TV and radio programs, and entertainment businesses across Texas, Oklahoma, and Florida. We had earned millions — but could not find the peace that comes only through knowing the truth.

Meeting a Muslim

In early 1991, my father began doing business with a man from Egypt — a Muslim. My first reaction? "No way." We had been told Muslims were terrorists, hijackers, people who "worship a black box in the desert." But then an idea came to me: "We can change this man to Christian."

I agreed to meet him — on my terms. I came carrying my Bible, a big shiny cross, and a cap that read: "Jesus Is Lord."

The Questions That Changed Everything

I asked Mohamed (the Egyptian) every question I knew: Do you believe in God? Adam and Eve? Abraham? Moses? The Ten Commandments? Jesus as the Messiah? To every single question — "Yes."

"Well now — this was going to be easier than I had thought. He was just about ready to be baptized — only he didn't know it. And I was just the one to do it, too."

Instead, day after day riding together on business trips across Texas, it was I who was being quietly asked questions I had no answers for.

A Priest and a Preacher Under One Roof

A Catholic priest named Father Peter Jacobs — a 12-year missionary in Latin America — came to recover at our home. Every night around the kitchen table: my King James Bible, my Revised Standard Version, my wife's copy, my father's, and the Catholic Bible (with 7 extra books). We spent more time arguing about which Bible was right than trying to convince Mohamed of anything.

One evening, Father Peter asked to visit the mosque with Mohamed. They came back late, after dark. I almost didn't recognize the priest — he was wearing a white robe and white cap.

"Pete — did you become a Muslim?" He said that he had entered into Islam that very day.

A Night That Changed Everything

My wife told me she too was entering Islam — she knew it was the truth. I went outside, found an old piece of plywood behind the house, and lay down with my head on the ground, facing the direction Muslims pray.

"O God. If you are there — guide me. Guide me."

No visions, no angels, no lights. Just a change inside me — a knowing that it was time to stop lying, stop the sneakiness, and live as an honest and upright man.

Around 11:00 A.M. the next morning, I stood before two witnesses — the former priest and Mohamed — and said my shahadah.

An Entire Household

My wife followed minutes later. My father — an ordained minister who helped build the Prayer Tower for Oral Roberts in Tulsa — declared Islam months later. Eventually the children, the stepmother — an entire household of Christians from three denominations — all came to Islam. One by one.

9 Steps to Seeking Truth

Sheikh Yusuf's advice to any sincere seeker:

  1. Clean your mind and heart — remove all prejudices
  2. Thank God for what you have, every moment
  3. Read a good translation of the Quran
  4. Reflect on the meanings and consider the bounties of your Lord
  5. Seek forgiveness from God and learn to forgive others
  6. Ask in your heart for guidance from Above
  7. Open your heart and mind
  8. Continue regularly for a few months
  9. Avoid the poison of evil while your heart is opening

— Chaplain Yusuf Estes, National Muslim Chaplain, Estes Heritage Foundation

The First American Islamic TV Channel

Launched in 2011 with a 48-hour live broadcast, Guide US TV became America's first Islamic satellite television channel. Now in its 14th year, streaming on Android, iOS, Roku, IPTV, and broadcast TV in key US cities.

During the very first live call-in show, a woman in Texas called to say: "I watched Guide US TV and now I want to do my shahadah!" Since then, thousands have come to Islam through the station.
